3. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

3.1 When to File

You should file a DMCA takedown notice if:

  • You own the copyright to content on our site
  • The content is used without your permission
  • The use does NOT qualify as Fair Use
  • You want the content removed

3.2 Required Information

To file a valid DMCA takedown notice, you MUST include the following information:

  1. Your Contact Information:
    • Full legal name
    • Mailing address
    • Phone number
    • Email address
  2. Identification of Copyrighted Work:
    • Description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed
    • If multiple works, a representative list
    • Proof of ownership (copyright registration, original files, etc.)
  3. Identification of Infringing Material:
    • Specific URL(s) where the infringing content appears
    • Description of where the content is located on the page
    • Screenshots (if helpful)
  4. Good Faith Statement:
    • "I have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."
  5. Accuracy Statement:
    • "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the owner."
  6. Physical or Electronic Signature:
    • Your handwritten signature (scanned) or electronic signature

3.3 How to Submit

Send your DMCA takedown notice to our designated Copyright Agent:

Response Time: Within 48 hours (business days)

4. Our Response Process

4.1 Review (24-48 hours)

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:

  1. Verify the notice contains all required information
  2. Review the claim and assess Fair Use applicability
  3. Contact you if additional information is needed

4.2 Action (48-72 hours)

If the claim is valid, we will:

  • Remove or Disable Access: Take down the infringing content
  • Notify the User: Inform the user who posted the content (if applicable)
  • Confirm Removal: Send you a confirmation email

4.3 Counter-Notice Process

If the user believes the content was removed in error, they may file a counter-notice. We will forward it to you and may restore the content after 10-14 business days unless you file a court action.

5. Counter-Notification (For Users)

5.1 When to File a Counter-Notice

If your content was removed due to a DMCA notice and you believe it was a mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notice.

5.2 Required Information

Your counter-notice must include:

  1. Your contact information (name, address, phone, email)
  2. Identification of the removed content and its location
  3. Statement under penalty of perjury that the content was removed by mistake
  4. Consent to jurisdiction of federal court
  5. Your physical or electronic signature

5.3 How to Submit

Send counter-notices to: dmca@aitoolshub.com with subject "DMCA Counter-Notice"

6. Repeat Infringer Policy

We have a policy of terminating accounts of users who are repeat copyright infringers. If a user receives multiple valid DMCA notices, we may:

7. False Claims

⚠️ Warning: Filing a false DMCA notice is perjury and may result in legal consequences, including damages and attorney fees under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f).

Before filing a DMCA notice, ensure:

  • You actually own the copyright
  • The use is NOT Fair Use
  • You are not abusing the DMCA process

8. Fair Use Considerations

Before filing a DMCA notice, consider whether our use qualifies as Fair Use under 17 U.S.C. § 107. Fair Use factors include:

  1. Purpose and Character: Is the use transformative? (e.g., review, commentary)
  2. Nature of Work: Is the work factual or creative?
  3. Amount Used: How much of the work is used?
  4. Market Effect: Does the use harm the market for the original?

Our use of AI tool logos and screenshots for review purposes typically qualifies as Fair Use.
